Overview

Samsung has made a significant impact on the smartphone industry with its Galaxy series, known for pioneering features like AMOLED displays, advanced camera technology, and high-performance processors. As a global leader in technology, Samsung has consistently pushed the boundaries of innovation, starting with the launch of its first Galaxy smartphone in 2009. The Galaxy series has redefined mobile communication with features such as the S Pen, foldable displays, and high-resolution screens. With each new release, Galaxy phones set new standards for innovation and quality, offering a wide range of models from budget-friendly to premium flagship devices, catering to a global audience. Learn more on the official Samsung website.

About

Founders Samsung was founded on March 1, 1938, by Lee Byung-chul as a trading company. Over the decades, it expanded into various industries, including electronics.
Current Leadership Samsung is currently led by Chairman Lee Jae-yong (also known as Jay Y. Lee), the grandson of the founder.
Industry Recognition Samsung is recognized as a global leader in electronics, particularly known for its innovations in smartphones, semiconductors, and display technology.
Global Influence Samsung is one of the largest and most influential companies in the world, particularly in the technology and consumer electronics sectors.
Key Products Samsung's major product lines include the Galaxy smartphones, QLED TVs, home appliances, and semiconductor products.
Market Capitalization Samsung Electronics has one of the highest market capitalizations in the world, often ranking among the top tech companies globally.
Retail Presence Samsung products are sold in over 200 countries, with a strong retail presence through both company-operated and third-party stores.
Brand Value Samsung consistently ranks as one of the most valuable brands globally, often featuring in the top five of global brand value rankings.
